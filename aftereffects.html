Adobe Premiere Pro

Adobe After Effects is a digital visual effect, motion graphics, and compositing application developed by Adobe Systems and used in the post-production process of film making, video games, and television production. Among other things, After Effects can be used for keying, tracking, compositing, and animation. It also functions as a very basic non-linear editor, audio editor, and media transcoder. In 2019, the program won an Academy Award for scientific and technical achievement.

I started using Adobe After Effects only in the summer of 2020. I still find it difficult to use, because the design seems non-intuitive to me and is different both from Adobe Premiere Pro and Vegas Pro. My experience with it is very small and I still don't know how to use many of its features. Despite that, I have successfully used it numerous times to generate a "sparks" effect, "blood splatter" effect, to hide my acne, and to generate a simple "synthwave" video.
